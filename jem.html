Jem, the Brown Bear

Jem, our zoo's brown bear, is our bears' big brother. He loves his three adopted siblings.

Jem was found abandoned as a cub and heavily injured. He was deemed a hazard if released into wild due to his injuries. Jem arrived at our zoo in March 2005 from Sancraz Zoo in Arizona. There are several brown bear subspecies found in Europe, Asia, and the Middle East, but they are smaller than their North American relatives and their numbers are currently so low that their populations are at critical risk. Jem is one of the oldest of our bears but certainly acts as the protective older brother. He is fond of lounging against a tree in the sun, watching his siblings at play.
