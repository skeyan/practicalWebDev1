Kim, the Black Bear

Kim, our zoo's black bear, is our youngest bear. She is gentle and loves honey.

Kim was found orphaned as a cub in Minnesota in April 2001. She was deemed a hazard if released into wild due to close interactions with humans. Kim arrived at our zoo in May 2010 from Hogle Zoo in Salt Lake City, Utah. The black bear is by far the most wide-ranging of the three bears that inhabit North America. In fact, these resourceful bears inhabit forests of at least 40 of the 50 states, including all states in the Southeastern U.S. Since coming into our care, Kim has been sweet and gentle. She loves honey and spending her time splashing in puddles.
